# Product Name & Mascot Exploration

> Brainstorm for a product name that supports character/mascot branding. Fox suggested by Zach as illustrative (not required). 10 candidates, top 3 developed fully.
> Working title being replaced: "Stride AI"
> Ruled out: Scout, Stride, Stride AI (by user)

---

## Criteria

1. **1–2 syllables** (3 max) — short names win
2. **Pronounceable globally** — no spelling traps
3. **Warm, humanlike** — a name you'd give a person or pet, not a SaaS tool
4. **Mascot-ready** — evokes an animal or character visual
5. **Running-appropriate** — doesn't conflict with the sport
6. **Not taken** by major running/fitness brands (quick check, Zach confirms availability)
7. **Brand-color compatible** — fits our Ember `#E94E1B` orange-coral
8. **Fox-compatible preferred** (per Zach) but not required if a stronger option exists

---

## The 10 Candidates

### 1. **Finn**
**Mascot:** Finn the Fox — a clever, wiry, warm-hearted red fox with alert ears and a long brush tail. Always one step ahead but never smug.
**Personality:** Sharp, curious, observant. Notices patterns you don't. Doesn't over-talk but chimes in when it matters.
**Why:** Finnish runners (Paavo Nurmi, Lasse Virén) are legendary. "Finn" is short, strong, warm. Works as app name AND coach name ("Finn thinks you should rest today.")

### 2. **Milo**
**Mascot:** Milo the Fox — slightly chubbier, softer fox. Friendly, patient, grounded. Wise old friend energy.
**Personality:** Kind, unhurried, methodical. Will tell you to go slower. Not above a bad pun.
**Why:** Milo of Croton was a legendary ancient Greek athlete — running culture tie. Warm, unthreatening name. Works cross-culturally.

### 3. **Remy**
**Mascot:** Remy the Fox — lean, alert, a touch mischievous. French fox energy.
**Personality:** Precise, a little witty, explains the "why" behind things. Less cuddly than Finn, more analytical.
**Why:** "Remy" echoes "remedy." French origin adds a bit of sophistication without being stuffy. Instantly character-able.

### 4. **Rory**
**Mascot:** Rory the Fox — Gaelic "red king." Confident, slightly mischievous, loves a challenge.
**Personality:** A bit more pushy than Finn/Milo. Celebratory, a little louder, more "let's go."
**Why:** Rory means "red king" in Gaelic — perfect fox name. Universally pronounceable. Athletic energy.

### 5. **Ember**
**Mascot:** Ember the Fox — a fiery, warm-toned fox that matches our brand color. Campfire-at-dawn energy.
**Personality:** Warm, patient, a slow-burn motivator. Not high-energy — steady and sure.
**Why:** We already have "Ember" as our brand color. Self-reinforcing naming. "Keep your ember burning" has an obvious metaphor for training consistency. Fox fits the color literally.

### 6. **Juno**
**Mascot:** Juno the Whippet / Greyhound — lean running dog (breed specifically bred for endurance + speed). Dignified, loyal, long-legged.
**Personality:** Calm, focused, steady. Less playful, more serious training partner.
**Why:** Juno (Roman goddess) connotes protection and guidance — the patron of athletes fits. Dog mascots have strong running association (Molly Seidel's coach Jon Green works with dogs, etc.).

### 7. **Kestrel**
**Mascot:** Kestrel the small falcon — fast, focused, sharp-eyed. Hovers with absolute control before striking.
**Personality:** Quiet, precise, patient. Speaks less, means more. Doesn't waste words.
**Why:** Birds of prey connote speed + focus. "Kestrel" is distinctive and memorable. Works for the data-focused, analytical runner. Less cuddly but cooler.

### 8. **Cinder**
**Mascot:** Cinder the Fox — dark orange/rust fox with soot-tipped paws. Campfire-at-dusk energy, paired with Ember.
**Personality:** Quieter, more introspective than Ember. Doesn't celebrate loudly, nods with satisfaction.
**Why:** Pairs naturally with our Ember brand color. "Cinder block" has that working-class grit. Distinct from the warmer Ember/Finn names.

### 9. **Tally**
**Mascot:** Tally the Fox — young, enthusiastic, counts things. Bushy tail, attentive eyes.
**Personality:** Curious, organized, energetic. Likes tracking things — fits the data obsession of runners.
**Why:** "Tally" suggests counting, tracking, accumulating. Maybe too cute for serious runners. Works better as a secondary coach/feature name than the product name.

### 10. **Copper**
**Mascot:** Copper the Fox — tangy, auburn fox. A little older, bit of gray at the muzzle. Seasoned.
**Personality:** Experienced, patient, been-there-done-that. Dry wit, doesn't overreact.
**Why:** Literally the color of a red fox. "Copper wire" has a slight tech/conductivity undertone that works. Veteran-coach energy.

## Top 3 Developed

### 🥇 1. Finn

**Name rationale:**
- 1 syllable, globally pronounceable, warm
- Running heritage (Finnish running legends: Paavo Nurmi — 9 Olympic gold medals)
- Works as app name AND personified coach (named by the user)
- Distinctive in the fitness space (most fitness apps have corporate names)

**Mascot — Finn the Fox:**
- Red fox species (Vulpes vulpes) — matches Ember `#E94E1B` brand color naturally
- Alert ears, intelligent eyes, long brush tail
- Tufts of cream-white fur on chest and tail tip
- Dark socks on legs (ink-brush quality feet)
- Slight forward lean — always watchful, always observant
- Approachable but not cartoonish — think closer to illustrated children's book fox than Disney fox

**Voice & Personality:**
- Sharp, observant, warm
- Notices patterns you miss ("Third Tuesday in a row your HR's been elevated after weights.")
- Asks questions before prescribing ("How'd that feel?")
- Doesn't over-talk — when Finn speaks, it's usually worth reading
- Occasional dry wit (never slapstick, never try-hard)
- Speaks with "let's" framing ("Let's keep today easy.")
- Never scolds; just notes what's going on

**Catchphrases / voice samples:**
- "Saw your run. Let's talk about it."
- "That's three good weeks in a row. Something's working."
- "Your body's asking for an easy day. Listen to it."
- "Nice. That pace surprised me — in a good way."
- "Let's dial this back 10%. Trust me."

**Visual direction:**
- Mascot illustrated in a warm editorial style (cut-paper or painterly — inspired by Alma)
- Orange-coral body with cream accents
- Used sparingly: empty states, onboarding moments, insight cards, 404 page
- NOT a dancing constantly-animated character — more like a logo + occasional spot illustration
- App icon: a simple fox silhouette or an "F" wordmark with fox-ear serifs

**App / coach naming:**
- App is "Finn" (the AI coach IS the product)
- User might say: "Finn said I should rest today"
- Emails come "from Finn"
- Tab labels stay generic ("Coach," "Dashboard") but proactive messages are signed with a small fox icon

**Why it wins:**
- Short enough to own the sound
- Warm without being cutesy
- Running heritage tie (Finnish running legends) adds credibility
- Fox is nailable visually at any size (favicon to hero)
- Available as brand space (many Finns exist but not in running specifically)
- Easier to build emotional attachment to than an abstract name

**Risks:**
- Extremely common name — domain likely taken. Try: getfinn.com, runwithfinn.com, finn.run, finn.coach, meetfinn.com
- "Finn" sounds similar to "Fin" (shark fin) — not a concern but noted
- Need to check for trademark conflicts in fitness/health

### 🥈 2. Ember

**Name rationale:**
- Already our brand color — self-reinforcing
- Literal metaphor for running: "keep your ember burning" (consistency), "fan the ember" (intensity when needed)
- Evokes warmth, fire, life — all athletic/running associations
- Distinct in the space (no major running app named Ember)

**Mascot — Ember the Fox:**
- A fiery orange-coral fox — color-matched to brand
- Slightly glowing warmth at the tail tip (subtle visual hint)
- Campfire-at-dawn energy — steady, enduring warmth
- Looks up with patient eyes, head slightly tilted

**Voice & Personality:**
- Warm, patient, a slow-burn motivator
- Less edgy than Finn, more steady encouragement
- Long-view thinker — talks about weeks and months, not days
- Gentle push when needed ("You've got more in you, but not today.")
- Emphasizes consistency over intensity

**Catchphrases:**
- "Slow and steady keeps the ember burning."
- "We're playing the long game. Today's just one day."
- "You did the work. Let the rest do its work now."

**Visual direction:**
- Fox mascot in full warm orange-coral
- Can incorporate subtle flame/spark visual elements (not literal flames — more abstract warmth)
- Logo could be a fox silhouette with a tiny spark mark
- App icon: glowing E mark or fox curl

**Why it works:**
- Double meaning (color + running metaphor) creates brand coherence
- Fox color matches literally — no need to explain why "Ember is a fox"
- Calmer emotional register than Finn — appeals to marathon / endurance runners who think long-term
- Tagline writes itself: "Keep your ember."

**Risks:**
- More abstract noun than a name — harder to personify as "Ember said..." (sounds slightly weird vs "Finn said...")
- The name "Ember" has some tech-company baggage (several startups use it)
- Might feel one-note if branding leans too hard into fire metaphors

**Compromise:** Use Ember as the PRODUCT name, give the COACH a name like "Finn" within the Ember product. But this splits identity. Simpler: pick one or the other.

---

### 🥉 3. Rory

**Name rationale:**
- Gaelic "red king" — literal fox name etymology
- Strong, athletic, unisex
- 2 syllables, easy to say, memorable
- Has "athletic celebrity" associations (Rory McIlroy, Rory Gallagher)

**Mascot — Rory the Fox:**
- Red fox, slightly larger/more confident than Finn
- Athletic build, muscular stance
- Head tilted in acknowledgment (not submissive) when listening
- Has a bit more dynamism than Finn — maybe mid-stride in the logo
- Slightly more mature-looking fox (adult, not kit)

**Voice & Personality:**
- Encouraging, a bit pushier than Finn
- Celebrates wins louder — "Nice 800! That's your fastest rep of the year."
- More "let's go" energy
- Direct, doesn't soften feedback as much
- Still companion-mode, but closer to an accountability partner than a meditative guide

**Catchphrases:**
- "Let's go. That's the session I wanted to see."
- "You held it. Next one, hold it cleaner."
- "Easy day. Actually easy. I mean it."

**Why it works:**
- Strong running/athletic energy
- Literal fox etymology (red king) makes the mascot inevitable
- Celebrity associations (Rory McIlroy) confer athletic legitimacy
- Works in group/team settings — "Our team's on Rory"

**Risks:**
- Maybe too athlete-coded (less gentle than our "companion" positioning wants)
- Celebrity association (McIlroy) might cause confusion
- "Rory" as a running brand — need to check trademark space carefully

## Decision Points for Zach

1. **Pick the name:** Finn, Ember, or Rory — or none (back to the drawing board)?
2. **Naming architecture:** Is the app named the same as the coach? E.g., "Finn" is both the app AND the AI coach's name — or is the app "Ember" and the coach is named "Finn" within it?
3. **Animal lock-in:** Fox for all top 3. Alternative animals worth exploring (wolf, greyhound, kestrel)?
4. **Domain / trademark search:** Before locking in, check availability of:
   - finn.run, finn.coach, getfinn.com, runfinn.com, meetfinn.app
   - ember.run, ember.coach, getember.com, emberrun.com
   - rory.run, rory.coach, getrory.com
5. **Illustration style for mascot:** Editorial/painterly (Alma-inspired) vs flat vector vs more detailed illustration?
6. **Mascot presence level:** Used sparingly (logo + occasional empty state / celebration) OR more prominent (animated buddy, always visible)?

## Recommendation

**Finn** is the strongest overall pick. It has:
- The clearest mascot direction (fox is a natural, immediate visual)
- Running heritage (Finnish runners)
- Warmth + strength balance
- Easy to personify as an AI coach ("Finn thinks you should...")
- Works at every size and context

**If you want more brand-color synergy, go Ember** — but accept that it's slightly more abstract as a name.

**If you want more athletic energy, go Rory** — but accept a slightly pushier personality.
